WORKFORCE CONNECT
Contractor Payment & Processing Guide

This document explains how compensation is handled for all service providers working with Workforce Connect. Please review carefully to avoid payment delays or misunderstandings.

1. Work Status

All individuals providing services through Workforce Connect do so as independent subcontractors.

This means:

You are not an employee

You are not on payroll

You invoice Workforce Connect through completed work

Because of this:

No CPP, EI, or income tax is deducted

You are fully responsible for declaring and paying your own taxes to CRA

2. Pay Cycle & Release Timing

Client-based work follows a bi-weekly reporting period.
However, payment is not released based on calendar dates.

Payments are issued only after:

Workforce Connect receives payment from the client

Once client funds clear, your portion is released immediately.

Processing speed depends on:

Client accounting schedules

Bank settlement times

Holidays and system maintenance

Proof of client payment may be requested for verification.

3. How You Get Paid

Workforce Connect supports the following payment methods:

A. Direct Deposit (EFT)
Requires a void cheque or direct deposit form

B. Interac E-Transfer
Subject to bank-imposed sending limits

C. Company Cheque
Available only for approved local contractors
